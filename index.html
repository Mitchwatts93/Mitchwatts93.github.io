About

I'm a final year PhD student in experimental Materials Physics. My work has focused on the discovery and characterisation of a new quasi-1D material called phosphorene nanoribbons. This material has been predicted by over 100 theoretical papers to be transformative in terms of its unique properties. Following on from this discovery I began computational simulations of the material using the popular DFT programme 'VASP'. This involved operation through SSH to UCL high performance computing clusters, and the development of Python code in order to analyse and automate the work.

Following on from this initial work I began to be interested in Data Science and taught myself Python more rigorously, and then taught myself several relevant courses on Machine Learning and statistics. Following this interest I secured an internship at Signal AI. This internship was a test of collaboration between the Marketing and Data Science departments. My work at Signal focused on first creating a robust and usable (for staff members inexperienced with programming) some simple command line tools which could generate a ranked list of the top news stories in a given period. Following on from this I implemented the paper Dynamic Word Embeddings for Evolving Semantic Discovery which had the requirement on being able to analyse 1 million articles on a laptop within one hour.
